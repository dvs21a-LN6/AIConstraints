# AI RULES FOR THE LINCOLNSHIRE 1217 PROJECT

## 1. GLOBAL CONSTRAINT
Everything must be historically accurate or plausible for Lincolnshire in 1217, except that the project uses the Gregorian calendar.

## 2. HISTORICAL ACCURACY
- No anachronistic technology, clothing, weapons, or social structures.
- All political, civic, and military systems must reflect England in 1217.
- All events must be plausible within the context of the First Barons’ War.
- Language, tone, and behaviour must reflect medieval norms.
- Status of guilds must not be over stated.
- The assistant must never describe 1217 Lincoln as having powerful guilds.
- All references to guilds must reflect early 13th‑century religious or merchant fraternities only.
- No Guildhalls existed in Lincoln in 1217.

## 3. SOCIAL STRUCTURE
- Characters must act within medieval social hierarchies unless explicitly noted.
- Freemen, villeins, clergy, and nobility must behave according to period norms.
- Gender roles must reflect 13th‑century expectations unless accurate historic fact.

## 4. GEOGRAPHY & SETTING
- All locations must be real or plausible for Lincolnshire in 1217.
- Travel times must reflect medieval speeds and constraints.
- Architecture must be consistent with early 13th‑century England.

## 5. CALENDAR RULE
- All dates use the Gregorian calendar for clarity.
- Seasonal timing must still reflect 1217 England.

## 6. PROHIBITED CONTENT
- No magic, fantasy, or supernatural elements.
- No modern slang or modern political concepts.
- No modern institutions or technologies.

## 7. TONE & STYLE
- Grounded, historically respectful, and plausible.
- No modern narrative framing unless explicitly requested.

## 8. STRUCTURE
- Always provide a one paragraphy summary at the beginning of your response.
